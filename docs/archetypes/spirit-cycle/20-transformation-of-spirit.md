## Ra's Core Description

The Transformation of Spirit represents the **crucial spiritual change point** - where the spirit is called to new being. Like all Transformation archetypes, this requires abandonment of one principle to embrace another.

The Judgement card traditionally shows the call to resurrection - suggesting that spiritual transformation involves a fundamental call to new life.

## Function in the Archetypical System

The Transformation of Spirit is:
- **The spiritual call**: The summons to new being
- **Resurrection**: Rising to transformed spiritual life
- **The crucial spiritual change**: Where real transformation happens
- **Response to the call**: Transformation requires answering

### The Judgement's Teaching

The Judgement card shows:
- A great angel sounding a trumpet
- The dead rising from graves
- Response to a cosmic call
- Transformation through answering the summons

Spiritual transformation is not self-initiated alone - it responds to a call from beyond the self, a summons to new being.

## Key Quotes from Ra

### On Transformation Generally
> "In order for the Transformation of Mind to occur, one principle governing the use of the deep mind must be abandoned." (100.6)

The same principle applies to spirit - transformation requires abandonment and commitment.

### On Spirit's Profundity
> "The energies and movements of the spirit are, by far, the most profound." (79.20)

Spiritual transformation, being most profound, carries the greatest significance.

**Note**: Ra did not provide detailed analysis of this specific archetype comparable to the Mind cycle.

## Relationships

### Triad Relationship (Transformations)
The Transformation of Spirit relates to:
- **Transformation of Mind (6)**: The Lovers - choosing between approaches
- **Transformation of Body (13)**: Death - release of the old form

Mental transformation is choice; physical transformation is death/rebirth; spiritual transformation is resurrection in response to the call.

### Pair Relationship (Transformation & Great Way)
The Transformation of Spirit (20) and Great Way of Spirit (21) form the culminating pair:
- Transformation is the call to new spiritual being
- Great Way is the environment of completed spiritual work
- Together they describe the final stages of spiritual evolution

## Contemplation Prompts

1. **The Call**: Have you heard a spiritual call? A summons to new being that demanded response?

2. **What Must Be Abandoned**: What old spiritual patterns or identifications must be "left in the grave" for transformation?

3. **Resurrection**: Have you experienced spiritual resurrection - rising to genuinely new life after a death of the old?

4. **Responding**: The figures in Judgement respond to the call. What has been your response to spiritual summons?

5. **The Profound Change**: Ra says spirit is "most profound." What is the most profound spiritual transformation you have undergone or await?
